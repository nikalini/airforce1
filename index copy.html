THE NIKE AIR FORCE 1

SINCE 1982

ABOUT NIKE

Nike is the most profitable shoe company in the world, but they do so much more than just shoes. With a wide range of products that range from shoes to clothes to equipment and more, Nike is a household name and has merchandise that all individuals can enjoy, no matter what the background. Nike is owned and worn by billions of people and continues to dominate the athletic apparel market to this day.

ABOUT THE SNEAKER

The famous Nike shoe, the Air Force 1, has defined all odds to become one of the best selling sneakers of all time. It is given the honor and title of being the first retro sneaker because it was created, then discontinued, then re-released a few years later, making it one of the first shoes to be brought back onto the market after its initial “failure”. Due to its popularity in the worlds of athletics, hip hop, and street style, the Air Force 1 has ingrained itself in the styles of various populations and demographics, allowing it to be a versatile sneaker that everyone can wear and style. The Air Force 1 has established itself as a CULTURAL ICON and can be seen all over the world today.

HISTORY OF THE AIR FORCE 1

1982

The Air Force 1 was designed by Bruce Kilgore and released to the public.

1983

The Air Force 1 is worn in the NBA by Moses Malone, Michael Cooper, Bobby Jones, Calvin Natt, Mychal Thompson, and Jamal Wilkes, also known as "The Original Six". In addition, Nike releases the first low version of the AF1.

1984

Nike decides to discontinue the Air Force 1.

1986

The Air Force 1 was re-released, making it the first ever retro sneaker.

Early 1990s

The Air Force 1 grows in popularity in major cities on the east coast.

Late 1990s

The Air Force 1 becomes a staple of the hip hop community and is reference many times by up and coming rap artists, including Jay-Z. Because of this exposure, sales for the Air Force 1 significantly increase.

2001

Women's Air Force 1 were released and different color combinations take over the market.

Mid 2000s

More and more variations of the Air Force 1 are released and with the introduction of NikeID, customers can customize their own Air Force 1s. Also, Nike began to work on Air Force 1 collaborations with rappers, like Nelly and Jay-Z, and other organizations, like Playstation.

2007

Rappers and artists Kanye West, Nas, Rakim, KRS-One, Rick Rubin, and DJ Premier record a song title "Classic" about the legacy of the Air Force 1 and perform it at the 25th anniversary celebration. The Air Force 1 is still at an all time high in the hip hop world.

2017

The 35th anniversary of the Air Force 1.
